Graveyard Epitaphs Urns

Mildred Abbatiello

Mildred had an unusual obsession with bubble wrap and found immense joy in popping the tiny air-filled bubbles. One fateful day, she received an oversized package containing an entire roll of industrial-grade bubble wrap. Overcome with excitement, Mildred decided to indulge in the ultimate popping experience by wrapping herself from head to toe in the bubble wrap. As she gleefully rolled around on the floor, reveling in the satisfying sound of each bubble bursting, she failed to notice the increasing tightness of the wrap around her body. Eventually, Mildred became completely enveloped in a cocoon of bubble wrap, unable to free herself and tragically suffocating amidst her beloved popping paradise.

Geraldine Aschenbrenner

Geraldine was a dedicated yoga practitioner who took her practice to extreme lengths. One morning, during her routine sun salutations on her apartment balcony, Geraldine attempted a particularly ambitious pose that involved balancing on one leg while holding a potted plant above her head. In her quest for perfect alignment, she lost her balance and toppled over the railing, landing in the neighbor's inflatable swimming pool. Despite the soft landing, Geraldine's demise was attributed to the shock of finding herself suddenly surrounded by floating flamingo pool toys.

Barry Mckinley

Barry fancied himself a master juggler with an affinity for performing daring feats in his suburban backyard. One sunny afternoon, he decided to up the ante by juggling various household items while simultaneously mowing the lawn. As he deftly tossed a set of steak knives into the air, Barry failed to notice the garden gnome hidden in the tall grass. In a comedic twist of fate, the lawnmower snagged the gnome and launched it into the air, striking Barry squarely on the head and rendering him unconscious. His neighbors later discovered him sprawled out on the lawn surrounded by rogue garden ornaments, a cautionary tale of the perils of multitasking gone awry.

Frank Bellavia-Cortezi

Geraldine's partner, Frank, couldn't resist flirting with the locals. Fed up, Geraldine sought revenge with a potion from Aunt Mildred, aiming for poetic justice. But when Mildred mistakenly added catnip instead of the intended herb, Frank's antics took a wild turn. He started purring, chasing imaginary mice, and ended up being mistaken for a lion by a passing the police and got killed.
